# Style Concept Prompt Set

## Shared Reference Roles

- Reference 1: responsive dashboard layout intent only.
- References 2 and 3: top-down pixel-art perspective, readable office-object scale, and material vocabulary only.
- The generated environment must be original and must not copy exact assets, colors, text, or layouts from the references.

## Shared Environment Brief

Create a wide landscape, top-down pixel-art, open-plan automation headquarters. Use one connected floor divided by rugs, furniture, lighting, plants, shelving, and low glass partitions rather than separate rooms.

Required zones:

- Research & Growth: four computer work seats.
- Creative Studio: two work seats plus camera and studio equipment.
- Release: three work seats for link, QA, and publishing work.
- Systems/NOC: one work seat plus server racks.
- Central mission/handoff area.
- Lounge, coffee, and mascot-resting area.

Global constraints:

- Exactly ten usable work seats.
- Clear, short, collision-free walking paths.
- Consistent top-down perspective, object scale, pixel density, and lighting.
- Environment only; no required characters.
- No readable text, logos, trademarks, watermarks, isometric perspective, or photorealism.
- Avoid fragmented rooms, maze corridors, blocked chairs, distorted equipment, inconsistent angles, pseudo-text, and copied branded assets.

## Concept A — Bright Operations

Mood: bright, calm, welcoming daytime operations headquarters.

Palette: cool pale blue and soft slate floors, warm oak desks, white walls, restrained navy and mint accents.

Priority: maximum readability, generous circulation, and neutral contrast for colorful agent sprites.

## Concept B — Tech Operations

Mood: focused evening operations center with practical task lighting; advanced but not a cyberpunk nightclub.

Palette: deep navy and charcoal floors, muted teal glass, warm amber task lights, restrained cyan/green status lights, medium walnut furniture.

Priority: compact team handoffs, strong NOC identity, and clearly differentiated work neighborhoods.

## Concept C — Warm Studio

Mood: warm, bright daytime creative automation studio; professional, optimistic, and calm rather than childish.

Palette: warm cream walls, honey oak and terracotta furniture, muted sage and teal rugs, soft blue-gray technology elements.

Priority: mobile readability, character contrast, and a comfortable management-sim atmosphere.

## Recommended Hybrid

Use Concept C for the base palette and mobile readability, Concept B for the compact Systems/NOC treatment, and Concept A for circulation and neutral operational clarity.
